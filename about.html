about the blog.

In Yabacau, I present some recent results in applied mathematics. I mostly discuss open problems that are found in contemporary literature, but I occasionally review classical results. If you find my mistakes, feel free to let me know.


Software and systems that I've built can be found on my projects page. These include analyses of datasets that interest me and tools that I've made to help others do the same. I'm open to collaboration.


Above all, Yabacau is a diary. It reflects the thoughts that I have during my spare time. I point to it when asked about my hobbies. Yabacau is a belief and a feeling. It pervades the mind, but it shies away from explanation. It's also a verb. I like to Yabacau at the end of a long week. If I could, I would Yabacau full-time.


Feel free to contact me about anything. You can find my information below.
